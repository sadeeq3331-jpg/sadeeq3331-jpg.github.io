1. Introduction to Endocrine Pharmacology

Endocrine pharmacology deals with drugs that either replace deficient hormones, inhibit excessive hormone production, or modulate hormone receptors. Many endocrine drugs are peptides (requiring injection), while steroids and small molecules are often oral.

  • Replacement therapy: levothyroxine, insulin, hydrocortisone.
  • Suppressive therapy: anti‑thyroid drugs, somatostatin analogs.
  • Hormone antagonists: tamoxifen, finasteride, mifepristone.

Pituitary Hormones
  • Growth hormone (somatropin): Used for GH deficiency, Turner syndrome, chronic renal failure. Side effects: fluid retention, glucose intolerance.
  • Mecasermin (IGF‑1): For severe GH insensitivity.
  • Pegvisomant: GH receptor antagonist; for acromegaly.
  • Desmopressin (DDAVP): ADH analog; used for central DI, nocturnal enuresis, hemophilia A (increases factor VIII).
  • Oxytocin: Stimulates uterine contractions (induction of labor) and milk ejection.
3. Thyroid & Antithyroid Drugs
Thyroid Hormone Replacement
  • Levothyroxine (T₄): Drug of choice for hypothyroidism. Half‑life ~7 days. Monitor TSH. Absorption reduced by food, calcium, iron.
  • Liothyronine (T₃): Rarely used; shorter half‑life; may be used in myxedema coma (IV).

5. Adrenal Hormones & Antagonists
Glucocorticoids
  • Hydrocortisone, prednisone, methylprednisolone, dexamethasone
  • Uses: Adrenal insufficiency, inflammatory/autoimmune disorders, asthma, allergic reactions, immunosuppression.
  • Adverse effects: Cushingoid features, hyperglycemia, osteoporosis, immunosuppression, adrenal suppression. Long‑term use requires slow taper.
Mineralocorticoids
  • Fludrocortisone: Used in adrenal insufficiency (Addison's) to replace aldosterone. Causes sodium retention, hypertension, hypokalemia.

7. Gonadal Hormones & Modulators
Estrogens & Progestins
  • Estrogen (ethinyl estradiol, conjugated estrogens, estradiol): Used in hormone replacement therapy (HRT), contraception. Adverse effects: thromboembolism, breast tenderness, endometrial hyperplasia (if unopposed).
  • Progestins (medroxyprogesterone, norethindrone, levonorgestrel): Used in contraception, HRT, endometriosis. Adverse effects: weight gain, depression, acne.
  • SERMs (Selective Estrogen Receptor Modulators):
    • Tamoxifen: Antagonist in breast, agonist in bone/uterus. Used for breast cancer; risk of endometrial cancer, hot flashes.
    • Raloxifene: Agonist in bone, antagonist in breast/uterus. Used for osteoporosis; lower risk of endometrial cancer; hot flashes.
Androgens & Anti‑Androgens
  • Testosterone (androgen replacement): For hypogonadism. Adverse effects: erythrocytosis, acne, sleep apnea, prostate stimulation.
  • Danazol: Weak androgen; used for endometriosis (now less common).
  • Anti‑androgens:
    • Finasteride, dutasteride: 5α‑reductase inhibitors → ↓ DHT; used for BPH, male pattern baldness.
    • Flutamide, bicalutamide: Androgen receptor antagonists; used for prostate cancer.
    • Spironolactone: Androgen receptor antagonist (also anti‑mineralocorticoid); used for hirsutism, female acne.
8. Drugs for Infertility & Menopause
  • Clomiphene citrate: SERM; blocks estrogen feedback → ↑ FSH/LH → ovulation induction. Side effects: hot flashes, multiple pregnancy.
  • Gonadotropins (FSH, LH, hCG): Used for ovulation induction and assisted reproduction. Risk of OHSS.
  • Cabergoline, bromocriptine: Dopamine agonists; lower prolactin → restore fertility in hyperprolactinemia.
  • Hormone replacement therapy (HRT): Estrogen ± progestin for menopausal symptoms. Risk: breast cancer, thromboembolism.
  • Ospemifene: SERM for dyspareunia due to vulvovaginal atrophy.

10. Drugs for Endocrine Neoplasia & Miscellaneous
  • Metyrosine: Inhibits tyrosine hydroxylase; used in pheochromocytoma (pre‑op).
  • Phentolamine, phenoxybenzamine: α‑blockers for pheochromocytoma (phenoxybenzamine non‑selective, long‑acting).
  • Vandetanib, cabozantinib: Tyrosine kinase inhibitors for medullary thyroid cancer.
  • Lutetium‑177 dotatate (PRRT): Peptide receptor radionuclide therapy for somatostatin receptor‑positive NETs.
  • Everolimus, sunitinib: mTOR inhibitor and TKI for pancreatic NETs.
