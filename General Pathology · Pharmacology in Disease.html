1. Anti‑Inflammatory Drugs
NSAIDs
  • Mechanism: Inhibit cyclooxygenase (COX‑1 and/or COX‑2) → ↓ prostaglandins.
  • Examples: Ibuprofen, naproxen, diclofenac, celecoxib (COX‑2 selective).
  • Uses: Pain, fever, inflammation (arthritis, gout).
  • Adverse effects: GI ulcers/bleeding (COX‑1), renal impairment, cardiovascular risk (COX‑2).
Corticosteroids
  • Mechanism: Bind glucocorticoid receptor, inhibit NF‑κB, reduce cytokine synthesis.
  • Examples: Prednisone, methylprednisolone, dexamethasone.
  • Uses: Autoimmune diseases, asthma, allergic reactions.
  • Adverse effects: Cushingoid features, osteoporosis, hyperglycemia, adrenal suppression.
Disease‑Modifying Antirheumatic Drugs (DMARDs)
  • Conventional: Methotrexate (DHFR inhibitor, adenosine increase), leflunomide (pyrimidine synthesis inhibitor), sulfasalazine.
  • Targeted: JAK inhibitors (tofacitinib, baricitinib).
2. Immunosuppressants
  • Calcineurin inhibitors: Cyclosporine, tacrolimus – inhibit T‑cell activation.
  • mTOR inhibitors: Sirolimus, everolimus – block IL‑2‑driven proliferation.
  • Antiproliferative: Azathioprine (purine antagonist), mycophenolate mofetil (IMPDH inhibitor).
  • Antibodies: Basiliximab (anti‑CD25), rituximab (anti‑CD20), alemtuzumab (anti‑CD52).

4. Chemotherapy Agents
  • Alkylating agents: Cyclophosphamide, ifosfamide, cisplatin – cross‑link DNA.
  • Antimetabolites: Methotrexate (folate antagonist), 5‑FU (thymidylate synthase inhibitor), 6‑MP (purine antagonist).
  • Antitumor antibiotics: Doxorubicin (intercalation, topoisomerase II inhibition), bleomycin (DNA strand breaks).
  • Plant alkaloids: Vinca alkaloids (vincristine, vinblastine – microtubule inhibitors), taxanes (paclitaxel, docetaxel – microtubule stabilizers).
  • Topoisomerase inhibitors: Etoposide (topo II), irinotecan (topo I).
5. Targeted Therapy
  • Tyrosine kinase inhibitors: Imatinib (BCR‑ABL, c‑KIT, PDGFR), gefitinib (EGFR), crizotinib (ALK).
  • Monoclonal antibodies: Trastuzumab (HER2), cetuximab (EGFR), rituximab (CD20).
  • mTOR inhibitors: Everolimus, temsirolimus.
  • PARP inhibitors: Olaparib (for BRCA‑mutated cancers).
  • Proteasome inhibitors: Bortezomib (multiple myeloma).
  • Immune checkpoint inhibitors: Ipilimumab (CTLA‑4), pembrolizumab (PD‑1), atezolizumab (PD‑L1).
6. Hormonal Therapy in Cancer
  • Breast cancer: Tamoxifen (SERM), aromatase inhibitors (anastrozole, letrozole), fulvestrant (ER antagonist).
  • Prostate cancer: GnRH agonists (leuprolide), anti‑androgens (bicalutamide, enzalutamide).
7. Antibacterial Agents
  • β‑lactams: Penicillins, cephalosporins, carbapenems, monobactams – inhibit cell wall synthesis.
  • Glycopeptides: Vancomycin – inhibit cell wall synthesis (Gram‑positives).
  • Aminoglycosides: Gentamicin, tobramycin – inhibit protein synthesis (30S).
  • Tetracyclines: Doxycycline – inhibit protein synthesis (30S).
  • Macrolides: Erythromycin, azithromycin – inhibit protein synthesis (50S).
  • Fluoroquinolones: Ciprofloxacin, levofloxacin – inhibit DNA gyrase/topoisomerase IV.
  • Sulfonamides & TMP‑SMX: Inhibit folate synthesis.
8. Antiviral Agents
  • Anti‑herpes: Acyclovir, valacyclovir – inhibit viral DNA polymerase.
  • Anti‑influenza: Oseltamivir (neuraminidase inhibitor).
  • Anti‑HIV: NRTIs (zidovudine), NNRTIs (efavirenz), protease inhibitors (ritonavir), integrase inhibitors (raltegravir).
  • Anti‑HBV/HCV: Entecavir, sofosbuvir, ledipasvir.
9. Antifungal Agents
  • Azoles: Fluconazole, itraconazole – inhibit ergosterol synthesis (CYP51).
  • Polyenes: Amphotericin B – binds ergosterol, forms pores.
  • Echinocandins: Caspofungin – inhibit β‑glucan synthesis.
  • Allylamines: Terbinafine – inhibit squalene epoxidase.
  • Flucytosine: Antimetabolite.
10. Antiparasitic Agents
  • Antimalarials: Chloroquine, artemisinin, mefloquine.
  • Anthelmintics: Albendazole, mebendazole, ivermectin.
  • Antiprotozoal: Metronidazole (Giardia, Trichomonas), pentamidine.
11. Anticoagulants & Antiplatelets
  • Heparins: Unfractionated, LMWH (enoxaparin) – activate antithrombin III.
  • Vitamin K antagonists: Warfarin – inhibit factors II, VII, IX, X.
  • DOACs: Dabigatran (thrombin inhibitor), rivaroxaban (factor Xa inhibitor).
  • Antiplatelets: Aspirin (COX‑1 inhibitor), clopidogrel (P2Y12 antagonist), abciximab (GP IIb/IIIa inhibitor).
12. Thrombolytics
  • Alteplase (tPA), streptokinase – convert plasminogen to plasmin, dissolve clots.
13. Lipid‑Lowering Agents
  • Statins: Atorvastatin, simvastatin – inhibit HMG‑CoA reductase.
  • Ezetimibe: Inhibits cholesterol absorption.
  • PCSK9 inhibitors: Evolocumab – increase LDL receptor recycling.
  • Fibrates: Fenofibrate – PPAR‑α agonists, lower triglycerides.
  • Niacin: Reduces VLDL.
14. Antidiabetic Drugs
  • Insulin: Various formulations.
  • Biguanides: Metformin – decreases hepatic gluconeogenesis.
  • Sulfonylureas: Glipizide – stimulate insulin secretion.
  • Thiazolidinediones: Pioglitazone – PPAR‑γ agonists.
  • DPP‑4 inhibitors: Sitagliptin – increase incretins.
  • GLP‑1 agonists: Liraglutide, semaglutide.
  • SGLT2 inhibitors: Empagliflozin – block renal glucose reabsorption.
15. Thyroid & Anti‑thyroid Drugs
  • Thyroid hormone replacement: Levothyroxine (T4), liothyronine (T3).
  • Anti‑thyroid drugs: Methimazole, propylthiouracil (inhibit thyroid peroxidase).
  • Iodine, radioactive iodine (¹³¹I).
16. Heavy Metal Chelators
  • Lead, mercury, arsenic: Dimercaprol, succimer (DMSA).
  • Lead (severe): Calcium EDTA.
  • Iron: Deferoxamine, deferasirox.
  • Copper (Wilson disease): Penicillamine, trientine.
17. Vitamins & Nutritional Supplements
  • Fat‑soluble vitamins: A, D, E, K.
  • Water‑soluble vitamins: B complex, C.
  • Indications: Deficiency states, supplementation.
