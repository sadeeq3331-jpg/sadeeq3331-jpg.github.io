1. Calcineurin Inhibitors
Calcineurin inhibitors block T‑cell activation by inhibiting the phosphatase calcineurin, preventing NFAT nuclear translocation and IL‑2 transcription.
DrugMechanismUsesToxicity
CyclosporineBinds cyclophilin → inhibits calcineurinTransplant rejection, rheumatoid arthritis, psoriasisNephrotoxicity, hypertension, hirsutism, gingival hyperplasia, hyperlipidemia
Tacrolimus (FK506)Binds FKBP12 → inhibits calcineurinTransplant rejection (liver, kidney), atopic dermatitis (topical)Nephrotoxicity, neurotoxicity (tremor), post‑transplant diabetes mellitus, alopecia

Monitoring: Therapeutic drug levels required due to narrow window and drug interactions (CYP3A4 inhibitors increase levels).

3. mTOR Inhibitors
  • Sirolimus (rapamycin), everolimus: Bind FKBP12 and inhibit mTOR → block IL‑2‑driven T‑cell proliferation.
  • Uses: Transplant rejection (often with calcineurin inhibitors), drug‑eluting stents (everolimus), tuberous sclerosis.
  • Toxicity: Hyperlipidemia, thrombocytopenia, delayed wound healing, mouth ulcers.
4. Corticosteroids
  • Examples: Prednisone, methylprednisolone, dexamethasone.
  • Mechanism: Bind glucocorticoid receptor, inhibit cytokine gene transcription, induce lymphocyte apoptosis.
  • Uses: Autoimmune diseases, allergic reactions, asthma, transplant rejection, lymphoma.
  • Toxicity: Cushingoid features, hyperglycemia, osteoporosis, immunosuppression, adrenal suppression (withdrawal).

6. Anti‑T Cell / Costimulation Blockers
  • Muromonab‑CD3 (OKT3): Anti‑CD3 monoclonal antibody; depletes T cells. Rarely used due to cytokine release syndrome.
  • Anti‑thymocyte globulin (ATG): Polyclonal antibodies against T cells; used in transplant induction and aplastic anemia.
  • Basiliximab, daclizumab: Anti‑CD25 (IL‑2 receptor α) monoclonal antibodies; used to prevent transplant rejection (no longer available).
  • Abatacept (CTLA‑4‑Ig): Inhibits CD28‑mediated costimulation; used in rheumatoid arthritis.
  • Belatacept: High‑affinity CTLA‑4‑Ig; used in kidney transplant (EBV seropositive only).
7. Anti‑B Cell Therapies
  • Rituximab (anti‑CD20): Depletes B cells; used in B‑cell lymphomas, rheumatoid arthritis, ANCA‑associated vasculitis, autoimmune hemolytic anemia.
  • Obinutuzumab, ofatumumab: Other anti‑CD20 antibodies for CLL, lymphoma.
  • Belimumab (anti‑BAFF): Inhibits B‑cell survival; used in SLE.
8. Intravenous Immunoglobulin (IVIG)
  • Pooled IgG from thousands of donors.
  • Mechanisms: Neutralization of pathogens, anti‑inflammatory via Fc receptors, idiotypic antibodies, complement inhibition.
  • Uses: Primary immunodeficiencies, ITP, Guillain‑Barré, Kawasaki disease, chronic inflammatory demyelinating polyneuropathy.
  • Risks: Infusion reactions, thrombosis, renal failure (sucrose formulations), aseptic meningitis.

10. Mast Cell Stabilizers
  • Cromolyn sodium: Prevents mast cell degranulation; used for allergic conjunctivitis (eye drops), asthma (nebulized), and mastocytosis.
  • Nedocromil: Similar to cromolyn; used in asthma and allergic conjunctivitis.
  • Ketotifen: Dual mast cell stabilizer and H1 antagonist; available as eye drops.
11. Leukotriene Antagonists
  • Montelukast, zafirlukast: CysLT1 receptor antagonists; used in asthma and allergic rhinitis.
  • Zileuton: 5‑lipoxygenase inhibitor; blocks leukotriene synthesis; less used due to hepatotoxicity and multiple doses.
12. Cytokine Therapies
  • Interferon‑α: Used for hepatitis B/C, melanoma, leukemia. Side effects: flu‑like symptoms, depression.
  • Interferon‑β: Used in multiple sclerosis; reduces relapses.
  • Interferon‑γ: Used in chronic granulomatous disease and osteopetrosis.
  • IL‑2 (aldesleukin): Used in metastatic renal cell carcinoma and melanoma; high toxicity (capillary leak syndrome).
  • Granulocyte colony‑stimulating factor (G‑CSF, filgrastim): Increases neutrophils; used after chemotherapy, in neutropenia.
  • GM‑CSF (sargramostim): Increases neutrophils, macrophages; similar uses.
13. Vaccines & Adjuvants
  • Live attenuated vaccines: Measles, mumps, rubella, varicella, yellow fever, BCG. Contraindicated in immunocompromised.
  • Inactivated/killed vaccines: Polio (IPV), influenza (inactivated), hepatitis A, rabies.
  • Subunit/recombinant: Hepatitis B, HPV, acellular pertussis.
  • Toxoids: Tetanus, diphtheria.
  • Adjuvants: Aluminum salts, MF59, AS04 – enhance immune response.
14. Immunotoxicities & Antidotes
  • Cytokine release syndrome (CRS): Associated with CAR‑T cells and certain antibodies (rituximab). Treatment: tocilizumab (anti‑IL‑6), corticosteroids.
  • Immune reconstitution inflammatory syndrome (IRIS): Seen in HIV after ART initiation; treat with corticosteroids if severe.
  • Anaphylaxis: Epinephrine (first‑line), antihistamines, corticosteroids.
  • Heparin‑induced thrombocytopenia (HIT): Stop heparin, use direct thrombin inhibitors (argatroban, bivalirudin).
