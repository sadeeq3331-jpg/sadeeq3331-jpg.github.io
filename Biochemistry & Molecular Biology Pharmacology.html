4. HMG‑CoA Reductase Inhibitors (Statins)
  • Mechanism: Competitive inhibition of HMG‑CoA reductase, rate‑limiting enzyme in cholesterol synthesis → ↑ LDL receptor expression → ↓ LDL‑C.
  • Examples: Atorvastatin, rosuvastatin (high‑intensity); simvastatin, pravastatin, fluvastatin (moderate).
  • Pleiotropic effects: Anti‑inflammatory, plaque stabilization.
  • Adverse effects: Myopathy, rhabdomyolysis (↑ risk with fibrates, gemfibrozil), hepatotoxicity.
5. ACE Inhibitors
  • Mechanism: Inhibit angiotensin‑converting enzyme → ↓ angiotensin II, ↓ aldosterone, ↑ bradykinin.
  • Examples: Captopril, enalapril, lisinopril, ramipril.
  • Uses: Hypertension, heart failure, diabetic nephropathy.
  • Adverse effects: Dry cough (bradykinin), angioedema, hyperkalemia, acute kidney injury in bilateral renal artery stenosis, teratogenic.
6. NSAIDs (COX Inhibitors)
  • Mechanism: Inhibit cyclooxygenase (COX‑1 and/or COX‑2) → ↓ prostaglandin synthesis.
  • Non‑selective: Ibuprofen, naproxen, indomethacin, ketorolac, aspirin (irreversible COX‑1/2).
  • COX‑2 selective: Celecoxib, etoricoxib.
  • Uses: Pain, inflammation, fever; low‑dose aspirin for cardioprotection (inhibits platelet TXA₂).
  • Adverse effects: GI ulcers/bleeding (COX‑1), renal impairment, cardiovascular risk (COX‑2), Reye syndrome (aspirin in children).
7. Protease Inhibitors
  • HIV protease inhibitors: Ritonavir, lopinavir, darunavir, atazanavir.
  • Mechanism: Inhibit viral protease → prevent cleavage of viral polyproteins → immature, non‑infectious virions.
  • Adverse effects: GI intolerance, hyperlipidemia, lipodystrophy, hepatotoxicity, drug interactions (CYP3A4 inhibition).

9. Urate‑Lowering Agents
  • Xanthine oxidase inhibitors: Allopurinol, febuxostat – ↓ uric acid production. Allopurinol hypersensitivity (HLA‑B*5801).
  • Uricosurics: Probenecid – inhibits URAT1 → ↑ uric acid excretion; avoid in uric acid nephropathy.
  • Recombinant uricase: Rasburicase – breaks down uric acid; used in tumor lysis syndrome (contraindicated in G6PD deficiency).
10. Osteoporosis Drugs
  • Bisphosphonates: Alendronate, risedronate, zoledronic acid – inhibit osteoclasts (farnesyl pyrophosphate synthase).
  • Denosumab: RANKL monoclonal antibody → ↓ osteoclast formation.
  • Teriparatide: PTH analog – anabolic (stimulates bone formation).
  • Selective estrogen receptor modulators (SERMs): Raloxifene – ↓ bone resorption.
11. Acetaminophen Poisoning
  • Acetaminophen is metabolized by CYP2E1 to NAPQI (toxic), normally detoxified by glutathione.
  • Overdose depletes glutathione → NAPQI accumulates → hepatotoxicity.
  • Antidote: N‑acetylcysteine (NAC) – replenishes glutathione, conjugates NAPQI.

13. Cyanide & Other Poisons
  • Cyanide: Binds cytochrome oxidase (complex IV) → histotoxic hypoxia. Antidote: Amyl nitrite, sodium nitrite (induce methemoglobinemia) + sodium thiosulfate (converts CN to thiocyanate).
  • Organophosphates: Inhibit acetylcholinesterase. Antidote: Atropine + pralidoxime (regenerates enzyme).
  • Carbon monoxide: Binds hemoglobin with high affinity → carboxyhemoglobin. Treatment: 100% O₂, hyperbaric oxygen.
14. Alkylating Agents
  • Add alkyl groups to DNA, causing cross‑linking and strand breaks.
  • Examples: Cyclophosphamide, ifosfamide, busulfan, melphalan, chlorambucil.
  • Toxicity: Myelosuppression, secondary leukemia, hemorrhagic cystitis (cyclophosphamide/ifosfamide → prevent with mesna).
15. Platinum Compounds
  • Cross‑link DNA similar to alkylators.
  • Examples: Cisplatin, carboplatin, oxaliplatin.
  • Toxicity: Cisplatin – nephrotoxicity (prevent with hydration), ototoxicity, peripheral neuropathy; carboplatin – myelosuppression; oxaliplatin – peripheral neuropathy (cold‑sensitive).
16. Topoisomerase Inhibitors
  • Topoisomerase I inhibitors: Irinotecan, topotecan – trap topo I‑DNA complex. Toxicity: diarrhea (irinotecan), myelosuppression.
  • Topoisomerase II inhibitors: Etoposide, teniposide, doxorubicin – trap topo II. Toxicity: myelosuppression, secondary leukemia (etoposide), cardiotoxicity (anthracyclines).
17. Microtubule Inhibitors
  • Vinca alkaloids (vincristine, vinblastine): Bind tubulin, inhibit microtubule polymerization. Toxicity: neuropathy (vincristine), myelosuppression (vinblastine).
  • Taxanes (paclitaxel, docetaxel): Promote microtubule stabilization, inhibit depolymerization. Toxicity: neuropathy, myelosuppression, hypersensitivity reactions.
