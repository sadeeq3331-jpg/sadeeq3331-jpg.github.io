1. Cholinergic Agonists
  • Direct acting (muscarinic): Bethanechol (urinary retention), pilocarpine (glaucoma, xerostomia), carbachol (glaucoma).
  • Indirect acting (anticholinesterases): Neostigmine (post‑op ileus, myasthenia), pyridostigmine (myasthenia), physostigmine (anticholinergic overdose), edrophonium (diagnosis of myasthenia).
  • Mechanism: Activate muscarinic or nicotinic receptors, or inhibit acetylcholinesterase.
2. Anticholinergic Drugs
  • Muscarinic antagonists: Atropine (bradycardia, pre‑anesthetic), scopolamine (motion sickness), ipratropium (COPD, asthma), tiotropium (COPD), darifenacin (overactive bladder).
  • Side effects: Dry mouth, blurred vision, constipation, urinary retention, tachycardia, confusion in elderly.
3. Adrenergic Agonists
  • Alpha‑1 agonists: Phenylephrine (nasal decongestant, hypotension), midodrine (orthostatic hypotension).
  • Beta‑2 agonists: Albuterol (asthma), terbutaline (tocolytic).
  • Non‑selective beta agonists: Isoproterenol (bradycardia, heart block).
  • Alpha and beta agonists: Epinephrine (anaphylaxis, cardiac arrest), norepinephrine (shock), dopamine (shock).
4. Alpha‑Adrenergic Antagonists
  • Non‑selective alpha blockers: Phenoxybenzamine (pheochromocytoma), phentolamine (hypertensive crisis).
  • Selective alpha‑1 blockers: Prazosin, doxazosin, terazosin (hypertension, BPH), tamsulosin (BPH only).
5. Beta‑Adrenergic Antagonists
  • Non‑selective (β1, β2): Propranolol, nadolol, timolol.
  • Cardioselective (β1): Metoprolol, atenolol, bisoprolol, esmolol.
  • With ISA: Pindolol, acebutolol.
  • With α‑blockade: Carvedilol, labetalol.
  • Uses: Hypertension, angina, heart failure, arrhythmias, migraine prophylaxis, glaucoma (timolol).
6. Antiarrhythmic Drugs (Vaughan Williams)
  • Class I (Na⁺ channel blockers):
    • Ia: Quinidine, procainamide, disopyramide (moderate blockade, ↑ QT).
    • Ib: Lidocaine, mexiletine (mild blockade, ↓ QT).
    • Ic: Flecainide, propafenone (strong blockade).
  • Class II (β‑blockers): Propranolol, metoprolol.
  • Class III (K⁺ channel blockers): Amiodarone, sotalol, dofetilide, ibutilide.
  • Class IV (Ca²⁺ channel blockers): Verapamil, diltiazem.
  • Other: Adenosine (AV nodal blocker), digoxin (Na/K‑ATPase inhibitor).
7. Antihypertensive Drugs
  • ACE inhibitors: Captopril, enalapril, lisinopril – cough, angioedema, hyperkalemia.
  • ARBs: Losartan, valsartan – no cough.
  • CCBs: Dihydropyridines (amlodipine, nifedipine) – vasodilation, edema; verapamil, diltiazem – negative chronotropy.
  • Thiazide diuretics: Hydrochlorothiazide, chlorthalidone.
  • Beta‑blockers.
8. Diuretics
  • Loop diuretics: Furosemide, bumetanide, torsemide – inhibit Na‑K‑2Cl cotransporter in thick ascending limb.
  • Thiazides: HCTZ, chlorthalidone – inhibit Na‑Cl cotransporter in distal tubule.
  • K⁺‑sparing diuretics: Spironolactone (aldosterone antagonist), eplerenone, amiloride, triamterene.
  • Carbonic anhydrase inhibitors: Acetazolamide – proximal tubule.
  • Osmotic diuretics: Mannitol – increase osmotic pressure.
9. Anticoagulants & Antiplatelets
  • Anticoagulants: Heparin (IV/SC), LMWH (enoxaparin), warfarin (oral, vitamin K antagonist), DOACs (dabigatran, rivaroxaban, apixaban).
  • Antiplatelets: Aspirin (COX‑1 inhibitor), clopidogrel (P2Y12 antagonist), abciximab (GP IIb/IIIa inhibitor).
10. Lipid‑Lowering Agents
  • Statins: Atorvastatin, simvastatin – inhibit HMG‑CoA reductase.
  • Ezetimibe: Inhibits cholesterol absorption.
  • PCSK9 inhibitors: Evolocumab, alirocumab.
  • Fibrates: Fenofibrate – PPAR‑α agonists, lower triglycerides.
  • Niacin: Reduces VLDL.
11. Drugs for Asthma & COPD
  • Bronchodilators: β2‑agonists (SABA: albuterol; LABA: salmeterol, formoterol), anticholinergics (ipratropium, tiotropium).
  • Anti‑inflammatory: Inhaled corticosteroids (fluticasone, budesonide), leukotriene antagonists (montelukast), cromolyn (mast cell stabilizer).
  • Monoclonal antibodies: Omalizumab (anti‑IgE), mepolizumab (anti‑IL‑5), dupilumab (anti‑IL‑4/13).
12. Drugs for Acid‑Peptic Disorders
  • Antacids: Aluminum/magnesium hydroxide.
  • H2 blockers: Ranitidine, famotidine, cimetidine.
  • PPIs: Omeprazole, esomeprazole, lansoprazole (inhibit H⁺/K⁺ ATPase).
  • Sucralfate: Coats ulcers.
  • Misoprostol: Prostaglandin analog, prevents NSAID ulcers.
13. Antiemetics
  • 5‑HT3 antagonists: Ondansetron, granisetron.
  • Dopamine antagonists: Metoclopramide, prochlorperazine.
  • NK1 antagonists: Aprepitant.
  • Antihistamines: Promethazine, diphenhydramine.
  • Anticholinergics: Scopolamine.
  • Cannabinoids: Dronabinol.
14. Antidiabetic Drugs
  • Insulin: Rapid‑acting (lispro, aspart), short‑acting (regular), intermediate (NPH), long‑acting (glargine, detemir).
  • Biguanides: Metformin (↓ hepatic glucose output).
  • Sulfonylureas: Glipizide, glyburide (↑ insulin secretion).
  • TZDs: Pioglitazone (↑ insulin sensitivity).
  • DPP‑4 inhibitors: Sitagliptin.
  • GLP‑1 agonists: Liraglutide, semaglutide.
  • SGLT2 inhibitors: Empagliflozin (↓ renal glucose reabsorption).
15. Thyroid & Anti‑thyroid Drugs
  • Thyroid hormone replacement: Levothyroxine (T4), liothyronine (T3).
  • Anti‑thyroid drugs: Methimazole, propylthiouracil (inhibit thyroid peroxidase).
  • Iodine & radioiodine: Iodine (Wolff‑Chaikoff effect), radioactive iodine (¹³¹I) ablates thyroid tissue.
16. Corticosteroids
  • Glucocorticoids: Prednisone, methylprednisolone, dexamethasone – anti‑inflammatory, immunosuppressive.
  • Mineralocorticoids: Fludrocortisone – for adrenal insufficiency.
17. Sex Hormones & Contraceptives
  • Estrogens: Conjugated estrogens, ethinyl estradiol (contraceptives, HRT).
  • Progestins: Progesterone, medroxyprogesterone, levonorgestrel.
  • Oral contraceptives: Combined estrogen‑progestin, progestin‑only.
  • Androgens: Testosterone.
  • Anti‑androgens: Spironolactone, bicalutamide, finasteride (5α‑reductase inhibitor).
18. Sedative‑Hypnotics
  • Benzodiazepines: Diazepam, lorazepam, alprazolam – enhance GABAₐ.
  • Z‑drugs: Zolpidem, zaleplon, eszopiclone.
  • Barbiturates: Phenobarbital, pentobarbital – increase duration of Cl⁻ channel opening.
  • Melatonin agonists: Ramelteon.
19. Antidepressants
  • SSRIs: Fluoxetine, sertraline, citalopram – first‑line.
  • SNRIs: Venlafaxine, duloxetine.
  • TCAs: Amitriptyline, nortriptyline – cardiac toxicity.
  • MAOIs: Phenelzine, tranylcypromine – dietary restrictions.
  • Atypicals: Bupropion (NDRI), mirtazapine (NaSSA), trazodone.
20. Antipsychotics
  • First‑generation (typical): Haloperidol, chlorpromazine – D2 antagonists; EPS, tardive dyskinesia.
  • Second‑generation (atypical): Risperidone, olanzapine, quetiapine, aripiprazole (partial agonist), clozapine (refractory schizophrenia; agranulocytosis risk).
21. Antiepileptic Drugs
  • Na⁺ channel blockers: Phenytoin, carbamazepine, oxcarbazepine, lamotrigine.
  • GABA enhancers: Benzodiazepines, barbiturates, tiagabine.
  • Ca²⁺ channel blockers (T‑type): Ethosuximide (absence seizures).
  • Multiple mechanisms: Valproate, levetiracetam, topiramate.
22. Drugs for Parkinson's Disease
  • Levodopa + carbidopa: (dopa decarboxylase inhibitor).
  • Dopamine agonists: Pramipexole, ropinirole.
  • COMT inhibitors: Entacapone, tolcapone.
  • MAO‑B inhibitors: Selegiline, rasagiline.
  • Anticholinergics: Benztropine, trihexyphenidyl (for tremor).
  • Amantadine: Antiviral with anti‑dyskinetic effects.
