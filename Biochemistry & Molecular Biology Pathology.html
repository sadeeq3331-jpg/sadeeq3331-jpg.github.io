2. Galactosemia
  • Classic (Type I): Galactose‑1‑phosphate uridylyltransferase (GALT) deficiency → galactose‑1‑phosphate accumulation.
  • Type II: Galactokinase deficiency → cataracts only.
  • Type III: UDP‑galactose‑4‑epimerase deficiency (may be benign or severe).
Presents after milk feeding: vomiting, diarrhea, jaundice, hepatomegaly, cataracts, E. coli sepsis. Treatment: lactose‑free diet.
3. Fructose Disorders
  • Essential fructosuria: Fructokinase deficiency; benign, fructose appears in urine.
  • Hereditary fructose intolerance (HFI): Aldolase B deficiency → fructose‑1‑phosphate accumulation → hypoglycemia, vomiting, liver failure after fructose ingestion. Avoid fructose/sucrose.
4. Pentose Phosphate Pathway Disorders
  • G6PD deficiency: X‑linked; impaired NADPH production → hemolytic anemia with oxidative stress (drugs, fava beans). Heinz bodies, bite cells.
5. Fatty Acid Oxidation Defects
  • Medium‑chain acyl‑CoA dehydrogenase (MCAD) deficiency: Most common; hypoketotic hypoglycemia, vomiting, lethargy, sudden death. Diagnose by acylcarnitines.
  • Very long‑chain acyl‑CoA dehydrogenase (VLCAD) deficiency: Cardiomyopathy, rhabdomyolysis.
  • Carnitine cycle defects: CPT1, CACT, CPT2 deficiencies → impaired fatty acid transport.

7. Lipoprotein Disorders
  • Familial hypercholesterolemia: LDL receptor mutation → ↑ LDL, tendon xanthomas, premature CAD.
  • Familial combined hyperlipidemia: ↑ LDL and VLDL.
  • Lipoprotein lipase deficiency: ↑ chylomicrons, eruptive xanthomas, pancreatitis.
  • Abetalipoproteinemia: Absent apoB → fat malabsorption, acanthocytes, neuropathy.
8. Phenylketonuria (PKU)
  • Classic: Phenylalanine hydroxylase deficiency → ↑ phenylalanine, ↓ tyrosine.
  • Features: Intellectual disability (if untreated), musty odor, eczema, hypopigmentation.
  • Maternal PKU: Maternal high phenylalanine causes fetal microcephaly, CHD.
  • Treatment: Low‑phenylalanine diet (avoid aspartame). Tetrahydrobiopterin (BH4) responsive in some.
9. Tyrosinemia
  • Type I: Fumarylacetoacetate hydrolase deficiency → liver failure, renal tubular dysfunction, neurological crises. Treated with nitisinone (NTBC) and diet.
  • Type II: Tyrosine aminotransferase deficiency → corneal ulcers, palmoplantar hyperkeratosis.
10. Homocystinuria
  • Most common: Cystathionine β‑synthase deficiency → ↑ methionine, ↑ homocysteine.
  • Features: Marfanoid habitus, intellectual disability, ectopia lentis (downward), thromboembolism, osteoporosis.
  • Treatment: Vitamin B6 (pyridoxine) responsive in some; methionine restriction, betaine.
11. Maple Syrup Urine Disease (MSUD)
  • Branched‑chain α‑ketoacid dehydrogenase deficiency → ↑ leucine, isoleucine, valine.
  • Features: Maple syrup odor in urine, ketoacidosis, neurological deterioration, seizures.
12. Alkaptonuria
  • Homogentisate oxidase deficiency → homogentisic acid accumulation.
  • Features: Dark urine on standing, ochronosis (bluish‑black pigmentation of cartilage), arthritis.
13. Urea Cycle Defects
  • Any enzyme deficiency leads to hyperammonemia, encephalopathy, respiratory alkalosis.
  • Ornithine transcarbamylase (OTC) deficiency: X‑linked; most common; ↑ orotic acid in urine.
  • Carbamoyl phosphate synthetase I (CPSI) deficiency: Requires N‑acetylglutamate; presents similarly.
  • Treatment: Protein restriction, sodium benzoate, phenylbutyrate, arginine supplementation.
14. Purine Disorders
  • Gout: Hyperuricemia → acute arthritis, tophi, urate nephropathy. Primary: overproduction or underexcretion. Secondary: increased cell turnover (leukemia), renal failure.
  • Lesch‑Nyhan syndrome: X‑linked HGPRT deficiency → ↑ uric acid, self‑mutilation, choreoathetosis, intellectual disability.
  • ADA deficiency: Adenosine deaminase deficiency → severe combined immunodeficiency (SCID).
15. Pyrimidine Disorders
  • Orotic aciduria: UMP synthase deficiency → orotic acid in urine, megaloblastic anemia, failure to thrive. (different from OTC where orotic acid also elevated).

17. Peroxisomal Disorders
  • Zellweger syndrome (cerebrohepatorenal syndrome): Peroxisome biogenesis defect → severe hypotonia, seizures, hepatic dysfunction, stippled epiphyses, early death.
  • Adrenoleukodystrophy (X‑linked): ABCD1 mutation → ↑ very long chain fatty acids → adrenal insufficiency, progressive neurodegeneration (childhood cerebral form).
18. Mitochondrial Disorders
  • Maternal inheritance; heteroplasmy; tissues with high energy demand affected.
  • MELAS: Mitochondrial encephalomyopathy, lactic acidosis, stroke‑like episodes.
  • MERRF: Myoclonic epilepsy with ragged red fibers.
  • Leigh syndrome: Subacute necrotizing encephalomyelopathy; bilateral basal ganglia lesions.
