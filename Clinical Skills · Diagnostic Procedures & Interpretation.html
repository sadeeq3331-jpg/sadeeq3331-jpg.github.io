1. Test Characteristics
Understanding diagnostic test performance is essential for interpretation.
  • Sensitivity: Probability that a test is positive when disease is present (TP / [TP + FN]). High sensitivity → good for ruling out (SnOUT).
  • Specificity: Probability that a test is negative when disease is absent (TN / [TN + FP]). High specificity → good for ruling in (SpIN).
  • Positive predictive value (PPV): Probability disease when test positive; depends on prevalence.
  • Negative predictive value (NPV): Probability no disease when test negative.
  • Likelihood ratio (LR): LR+ = sensitivity/(1‑specificity); LR‑ = (1‑sensitivity)/specificity. Post‑test odds = pretest odds × LR.
2. Laboratory Specimens & Handling
  • Serum: Clotted blood (red top).
  • Plasma: Anticoagulated (lavender EDTA, green heparin, blue citrate).
  • Urine: Clean catch, midstream; 24‑hour collection.
  • CSF: Collected via lumbar puncture; tubes for cell count, chemistry, microbiology.
  • Blood culture: Aseptic technique, 2‑3 sets.
3. Complete Blood Count (CBC)
  • RBC indices: MCV (micro‑/normo‑/macrocytic), MCH, MCHC, RDW.
  • WBC count and differential: Neutrophils (↑ in bacterial infection), lymphocytes (↑ viral, ↓ HIV), eosinophils (allergy, parasites), monocytes, basophils.
  • Platelets: Thrombocytopenia (<150) or thrombocytosis.
4. Comprehensive Metabolic Panel (CMP)
  • Electrolytes: Na, K, Cl, HCO₃. Calculate anion gap = Na – (Cl + HCO₃) (normal 8‑12).
  • Renal function: BUN, creatinine (eGFR).
  • Glucose.
  • Calcium, albumin, total protein.
  • Liver enzymes: AST, ALT, ALP, bilirubin (total, direct).
5. Coagulation Studies
  • PT/INR: Extrinsic pathway (factor VII, vitamin K).
  • PTT: Intrinsic pathway (factors VIII, IX, XI, XII).
  • Fibrinogen, D‑dimer.
6. Cardiac Biomarkers
  • Troponin I/T: Most specific for myocardial injury; rise 4‑6h, peak 12‑24h.
  • CK‑MB: Less specific, earlier rise (4‑6h).
  • BNP/NT‑proBNP: Heart failure; >100 pg/mL suggests HF.
7. Inflammatory Markers
  • ESR: Non‑specific; elevated in inflammation, infection, malignancy.
  • CRP: Acute‑phase reactant; highly sensitive; bacterial infection, inflammation.
8. Urinalysis
  • Dipstick: pH, specific gravity, protein, glucose, ketones, blood, leukocyte esterase, nitrites.
  • Microscopy: RBCs (hematuria), WBCs (pyuria), casts (RBC casts → glomerulonephritis; WBC casts → pyelonephritis), crystals.

10. Pleural, Ascitic, Synovial Fluid
  • Pleural fluid: Light's criteria (exudative vs transudative).
  • Ascitic fluid: SAAG (serum‑ascites albumin gradient) >1.1 g/dL indicates portal hypertension.
  • Synovial fluid: WBC count for septic (>50,000), inflammatory (2,000‑50,000), non‑inflammatory (<2,000).
11. Plain Radiography (X‑Ray)
  • Chest X‑ray (CXR): Evaluate lung fields, heart size, mediastinum, pleural spaces.
  • Abdominal X‑ray: Bowel obstruction, free air, kidney stones.
  • Bone X‑ray: Fractures, arthritis, osteomyelitis.
12. Computed Tomography (CT)
  • Cross‑sectional imaging; with or without contrast.
  • Excellent for trauma, brain hemorrhage, appendicitis, pulmonary embolism (CTPA), renal stones (non‑contrast).
13. Ultrasound
  • Real‑time, no radiation, excellent for soft tissue, vascular (Doppler), cardiac (echo), abdominal (gallstones, hydronephrosis), OB/GYN.
14. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI)
  • Superior soft tissue contrast; brain, spine, joints, soft tissue tumors.
  • Contraindications: pacemakers, certain implants.
15. Nuclear Medicine
  • PET scan: Metabolic activity (cancer, infection).
  • Bone scan: Metastases, osteomyelitis.
  • V/Q scan: Pulmonary embolism.
16. ECG Basics & Interpretation
  • P wave: Atrial depolarization.
  • PR interval: 120‑200 ms; AV node conduction.
  • QRS complex: Ventricular depolarization; normal <120 ms.
  • QT interval: Corrected for heart rate (QTc); prolonged → torsades risk.
  • ST segment, T wave: Ventricular repolarization.
17. ECG in Arrhythmias
  • Sinus rhythm: P before each QRS, rate 60‑100.
  • Atrial fibrillation: Irregularly irregular, no P waves.
  • Atrial flutter: Sawtooth baseline.
  • VT: Wide QRS, rate >100, AV dissociation.
  • AV blocks: 1st (PR >200), 2nd Mobitz I (Wenckebach, progressive PR until dropped QRS), Mobitz II (constant PR, dropped beats), 3rd (complete block).
18. ECG in Ischemia & Infarction
  • Ischemia: ST depression, T wave inversion.
  • Injury: ST elevation (transmural).
  • Infarction (Q wave): Pathologic Q waves >1 box wide, >1/3 QRS height.
  • Localization:
    • Anterior: V1‑V4
    • Lateral: I, aVL, V5‑V6
    • Inferior: II, III, aVF
    • Posterior: ST depression V1‑V3, tall R, R/S >1
19. Pulmonary Function Tests
  • Spirometry: FVC, FEV₁, FEV₁/FVC ratio. Obstructive (↓ ratio), restrictive (↓ FVC, normal/↑ ratio).
  • Lung volumes: TLC, RV, RV/TLC.
  • DLCO: Diffusing capacity; ↓ in emphysema, fibrosis, anemia.
20. Holter & Event Monitoring
  • Holter: 24‑48h continuous ECG; detect arrhythmias, ST changes.
  • Event monitor: Patient‑activated, worn for weeks; records when symptomatic.
21. Biopsy & Cytology
  • Fine needle aspiration (FNA): Cells aspirated; for masses (thyroid, lymph nodes).
  • Core needle biopsy: Tissue core; preserves architecture.
  • Excisional biopsy: Entire lesion removed.
  • Cytology: Pap smear, urine cytology, pleural fluid.
