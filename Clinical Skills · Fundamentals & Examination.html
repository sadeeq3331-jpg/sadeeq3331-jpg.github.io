1. Medical History Taking
A thorough medical history is the foundation of clinical diagnosis. Components:
  • Identifying data: Age, gender, occupation, race/ethnicity.
  • Chief complaint: Patient's own words describing the reason for visit.
  • History of present illness (HPI): Chronologic narrative, using OLD CARTS (Onset, Location, Duration, Character, Aggravating/Associated factors, Relieving factors, Timing, Severity).
  • Past medical history: Medical conditions, surgeries, hospitalizations, psychiatric history.
  • Medications and allergies: Prescription, OTC, supplements; drug allergies.
  • Family history: Genetic diseases, heart disease, diabetes, cancer.
  • Social history: Smoking, alcohol, drugs, living situation, occupation.
  • Review of systems (ROS): Systematic checklist of symptoms by organ system.
Mnemonic for HPI: OLD CARTS (Onset, Location, Duration, Character, Aggravating/Associated, Relieving, Timing, Severity).
2. Vital Signs
  • Temperature: Normal 36.5‑37.5°C (oral). Hyperthermia >38°C, hypothermia <35°C.
  • Heart rate: 60‑100 bpm. Tachycardia >100, bradycardia <60.
  • Respiratory rate: 12‑20 breaths/min. Tachypnea >20.
  • Blood pressure: Normal <120/80 mmHg. Hypertension ≥130/80, hypotension <90/60.
  • Oxygen saturation: ≥95% on room air.
  • Pain: 5th vital sign; 0‑10 scale.
Orthostatic vitals: Measure BP and HR supine, then after 1‑3 minutes standing. Positive if systolic drop >20 mmHg, diastolic drop >10, or HR increase >20.
3. General Survey & Appearance
  • Appearance: Age, gender, body habitus, hygiene, grooming.
  • Level of consciousness: Alert, lethargic, obtunded, stuporous, comatose.
  • Distress: Respiratory distress, pain, anxiety.
  • Nutritional status: Obese, cachectic, muscular.
  • Posture and gait: Abnormal postures, limping, ataxia.
4. Head & Face
  • Inspection: Symmetry, deformities, scalp lesions, facial expression.
  • Palpation: Temporomandibular joint (TMJ) tenderness, crepitus; sinuses tenderness.
  • Facial nerve (CN VII): Raise eyebrows, close eyes tightly, smile, puff cheeks.
5. Eye Examination
  • Visual acuity: Snellen chart; test each eye separately.
  • Visual fields: Confrontation; test all quadrants.
  • Extraocular movements (CN III, IV, VI): H‑pattern; nystagmus.
  • Pupils: Size, symmetry, direct and consensual light reflex, accommodation.
  • Ophthalmoscopy: Red reflex, optic disc (cup, margins), vessels, retina.
  • External: Eyelids, conjunctiva, sclera, cornea.
Marcus Gunn pupil (relative afferent pupillary defect): Swinging flashlight test – pupil dilates when light moves to affected eye.
6. Ear Examination
  • Inspection: Pinna, external auditory canal, tympanic membrane (cone of light, landmarks).
  • Otoscopy: Perforation, erythema, effusion, cerumen.
  • Hearing (CN VIII): Whisper test, Rinne (air > bone), Weber (lateralization).
7. Nose & Sinuses
  • Inspection: External nose, nasal mucosa, septum, turbinates.
  • Palpation: Frontal and maxillary sinuses for tenderness.
  • Olfaction (CN I): Test with coffee, peppermint.
8. Mouth & Throat
  • Lips: Color, moisture, lesions.
  • Oral mucosa: Ulcers, white patches, pigmentation.
  • Teeth and gums: Caries, periodontal disease, bleeding.
  • Palate: Intact, symmetry (CN IX, X).
  • Tongue (CN XII): Protrusion (midline), atrophy, fasciculations.
  • Oropharynx: Tonsils, posterior pharynx; assess gag reflex (CN IX, X).
9. Neck & Thyroid
  • Inspection: Symmetry, scars, masses, jugular venous distention.
  • Palpation: Trachea (midline), cervical lymph nodes, thyroid (isthmus, lobes).
  • Auscultation: Carotid bruits, thyroid bruit.
  • Range of motion: Flexion, extension, rotation, lateral bending.
10. Respiratory Examination
Sequence: inspection, palpation, percussion, auscultation.
  • Inspection: Chest shape (barrel, pectus), symmetry of expansion, retractions, use of accessory muscles.
  • Palpation: Tactile fremitus (increased in consolidation, decreased in effusion/pneumothorax), tracheal deviation.
  • Percussion: Resonant (normal), dull (consolidation, effusion), hyperresonant (COPD, pneumothorax).
  • Auscultation: Breath sounds (vesicular, bronchial, bronchovesicular), adventitious sounds (crackles, wheezes, rhonchi, pleural rub).
  • Vocal resonance: Bronchophony, egophony (E to A), whispered pectoriloquy.
11. Cardiac Examination
  • Inspection and palpation: Point of maximal impulse (PMI) location, thrills, heaves.
  • Auscultation:
    • Heart sounds: S1 (mitral/tricuspid closure), S2 (aortic/pulmonic closure).
    • S3: Ventricular gallop (heart failure).
    • S4: Atrial gallop (hypertrophy).
    • Murmurs: Timing (systolic/diastolic), location, radiation, intensity (1‑6), pitch, quality.
    • Pericardial rub: Scratching sound, positional.
  • Jugular venous pressure (JVP): Estimate at 45°, normally ≤3 cm above sternal angle.
12. Abdominal Examination
  • Inspection: Contour (flat, scaphoid, distended), scars, hernias, visible peristalsis, pulsations.
  • Auscultation: Bowel sounds (absent, hyperactive), bruits (renal, aortic, iliac).
  • Percussion: Tympany (gas), dullness (fluid, mass). Liver span (6‑12 cm), spleen (Traube's space dullness).
  • Palpation: Light (tenderness, guarding), deep (masses, organomegaly). Liver, spleen, kidneys.
  • Special maneuvers: Murphy sign (cholecystitis), McBurney point tenderness (appendicitis), rebound tenderness, obturator/psoas signs.
13. Musculoskeletal Examination
  • Inspection: Joint swelling, deformity, erythema, muscle atrophy.
  • Palpation: Tenderness, warmth, crepitus.
  • Range of motion: Active and passive; note limitations.
  • Muscle strength: 0‑5 scale.
  • Specific tests: Lachman (ACL), McMurray (meniscus), Phalen (carpal tunnel), Tinel sign.
14. Neurological Examination
  • Mental status: Alertness, orientation, memory, language, attention (MMSE/MoCA).
  • Cranial nerves I‑XII.
  • Motor system: Muscle bulk, tone (rigidity, spasticity), power, coordination (finger‑nose, heel‑shin).
  • Sensory: Light touch, pain, temperature, vibration, proprioception; cortical sensation.
  • Reflexes: Deep tendon reflexes (biceps, triceps, patellar, Achilles), Babinski, clonus.
  • Gait: Normal, tandem, heel/toe walking.
15. Peripheral Vascular Examination
  • Palpation of pulses: Carotid, brachial, radial, femoral, popliteal, dorsalis pedis, posterior tibial. Grade 0‑4+.
  • Auscultation for bruits: Carotid, aorta, femoral.
  • Capillary refill: <2 seconds.
  • Edema: Pitting vs non‑pitting; grade 1‑4.
  • Signs of venous insufficiency: Varicosities, stasis dermatitis, ulcers (medial malleolus).
  • Arterial insufficiency: Pallor on elevation, rubor on dependency, ulcers (toes, heel).
16. Skin Examination
  • Inspection: Color, lesions (macule, papule, nodule, vesicle, bulla, pustule, plaque, scale, erosion, ulcer), distribution.
  • Palpation: Texture, temperature, moisture, turgor.
  • Specific lesions: Purpura (non‑blanching), petechiae, ecchymosis, telangiectasias.
  • Hair and nails: Alopecia, nail pitting, clubbing, koilonychia.
