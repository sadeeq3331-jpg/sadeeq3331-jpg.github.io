Practical implications: Water‑soluble drugs have larger Vd in infants; fat‑soluble drugs may have prolonged half‑life in older children. Renal clearance matures over first year; dose intervals often need adjustment.

2. Drug Dosing & Calculations
  • Weight‑based dosing (mg/kg): Most common, but maximum doses apply (especially for acetaminophen).
  • Body surface area (BSA): Used for chemotherapy, some cardiac drugs; Mosteller formula: BSA (m²) = √(height(cm) × weight(kg) / 3600).
  • Age‑based dosing: Sometimes used for vaccines, but weight is preferred.
Example – Paracetamol (acetaminophen): 15 mg/kg/dose q4‑6h (max 60 mg/kg/day). For a 10 kg child: 150 mg per dose.
3. Neonatal Drug Handling
  • Chloramphenicol: Immature glucuronidation → gray baby syndrome (cardiovascular collapse, cyanosis).
  • Sulfonamides: Displace bilirubin from albumin → kernicterus risk. Avoid in neonates.
  • Morphine: Increased CNS sensitivity; reduced clearance → risk of respiratory depression.
  • Phenobarbital: Used for neonatal seizures; long half‑life.
4. Vitamin K Prophylaxis
Newborns are deficient in vitamin K due to poor placental transfer, sterile gut, and low breast milk content. Intramuscular vitamin K (0.5‑1 mg) given at birth prevents hemorrhagic disease of the newborn (HDN).
5. Erythropoietin & Anemia
Recombinant human erythropoietin (epoetin alfa) is used in preterm infants to reduce transfusion needs. Also used in chronic renal failure.

7. Cardiac Drugs
  • Prostaglandin E1 (alprostadil): Maintains ductus arteriosus patency in duct‑dependent lesions (e.g., transposition, pulmonary atresia).
  • Indomethacin / ibuprofen: Promote closure of PDA in preterm infants (inhibit prostaglandin synthesis). Side effects: renal impairment, necrotizing enterocolitis, bleeding.
  • Digoxin: Used in heart failure, supraventricular tachycardia. Narrow therapeutic index; toxicity (nausea, arrhythmias).
  • Diuretics (furosemide, spironolactone): Manage fluid overload in CHD.
8. Gastrointestinal Drugs
  • H2 blockers (ranitidine) / PPI (omeprazole): GERD in infants. Omeprazole dose: 0.7‑3.5 mg/kg/day.
  • Metoclopramide: Prokinetic; risk of extrapyramidal side effects, use cautiously.
  • Lactulose / polyethylene glycol: Constipation.
  • Pancreatic enzyme replacement: Cystic fibrosis.
9. Renal & Fluid/Electrolytes
  • Diuretics: Furosemide (loop) for pulmonary edema; spironolactone (potassium‑sparing).
  • ACE inhibitors (enalapril, captopril): Hypertension, heart failure. Risk of hyperkalemia, renal impairment.
  • IV fluids: Maintenance: D5 1/4NS or D5 1/2NS with KCl. Dehydration: oral rehydration solution (ORS) or IVF (NS, LR).
10. CNS Drugs
  • Antiepileptics:
    • Phenobarbital: Neonatal seizures (load 20 mg/kg).
    • Phenytoin / fosphenytoin: Broad spectrum, can cause gingival hyperplasia.
    • Levetiracetam: Increasingly used, fewer interactions.
    • Valproate: Avoid in young females (hepatotoxicity, teratogenicity).
  • ADHD medications: Methylphenidate, amphetamine salts, atomoxetine (non‑stimulant).
  • Antidepressants: Fluoxetine (first‑line for pediatric depression/anxiety); monitor for suicidality.
11. Endocrine Drugs
  • Insulin: Type 1 diabetes; rapid‑acting (lispro, aspart), long‑acting (glargine).
  • Growth hormone (somatropin): GH deficiency, Turner syndrome, chronic renal failure.
  • Levothyroxine: Congenital hypothyroidism – crucial for neurodevelopment.
  • Hydrocortisone: Congenital adrenal hyperplasia (replacement).

13. Antivirals
  • Acyclovir: HSV encephalitis, neonatal HSV, varicella (severe). Dose: 10‑20 mg/kg/dose IV q8h.
  • Oseltamivir: Influenza (treatment and prophylaxis).
  • Ganciclovir: CMV disease (e.g., congenital CMV).
14. Antifungals
  • Nystatin: Oral thrush (topical).
  • Fluconazole: Systemic candidiasis; good CSF penetration.
  • Amphotericin B (liposomal): Invasive fungal infections (e.g., aspergillosis, candidiasis).

16. Pain & Fever Management
  • Acetaminophen (paracetamol): 15 mg/kg/dose q4‑6h; max 60 mg/kg/day. Avoid >75 mg/kg/day due to hepatotoxicity.
  • Ibuprofen: 10 mg/kg/dose q6‑8h; max 40 mg/kg/day. Contraindicated in dehydration (renal risk).
  • Naproxen: Juvenile idiopathic arthritis.
  • Opioids (morphine, fentanyl): Severe pain, post‑op; monitor respiratory status.

18. Immunomodulators
  • Systemic corticosteroids: Asthma, inflammatory bowel disease, rheumatic disorders.
  • Methotrexate (low dose): Juvenile idiopathic arthritis.
  • Biologics (adalimumab, etanercept): JIA, uveitis, IBD.
  • IVIG: Kawasaki disease, immune thrombocytopenia, Guillain‑Barré.
