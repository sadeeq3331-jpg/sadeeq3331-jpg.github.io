1. Amino Acids & Proteins
Amino acids: 20 standard amino acids; classified by side chain (nonpolar, polar uncharged, acidic, basic). Essential amino acids: PVT TIM HALL (Phe, Val, Thr, Trp, Ile, Met, His, Arg, Leu, Lys) – Arg, His essential in children.
  • Peptide bond: Formed by condensation between α‑carboxyl and α‑amino group.
  • Protein structure: Primary (sequence), secondary (α‑helix, β‑sheet), tertiary (3D folding), quaternary (multisubunit).
  • Post‑translational modifications: Phosphorylation, glycosylation, ubiquitination.
2. Enzymes & Kinetics
  • Enzymes lower activation energy, are not consumed.
  • Michaelis‑Menten kinetics: Vmax (maximum velocity), Km (substrate concentration at half Vmax; affinity inversely related).
  • Lineweaver‑Burk plot: 1/V vs 1/[S]; intercepts give 1/Vmax and −1/Km.
  • Inhibition: Competitive (↑ Km, same Vmax), noncompetitive (↓ Vmax, same Km), uncompetitive (both ↓).
  • Regulation: Allosteric (feedback inhibition), covalent modification (phosphorylation), zymogen activation.
3. Glycolysis
  • Cytosolic pathway: Glucose → 2 pyruvate; net 2 ATP, 2 NADH.
  • Key enzymes: Hexokinase/glucokinase, phosphofructokinase‑1 (PFK‑1; rate‑limiting, activated by AMP, fructose‑2,6‑BP; inhibited by ATP, citrate), pyruvate kinase.
  • Anaerobic: Pyruvate → lactate (LDH).
4. Gluconeogenesis
  • Primarily liver; synthesis of glucose from non‑carbohydrate precursors (lactate, glycerol, glucogenic amino acids).
  • Key enzymes bypassing glycolysis irreversible steps: Pyruvate carboxylase (mitochondria), PEP carboxykinase, fructose‑1,6‑bisphosphatase, glucose‑6‑phosphatase.
5. Glycogen Metabolism
  • Glycogen synthesis: UDP‑glucose + glycogenin; glycogen synthase (rate‑limiting).
  • Glycogenolysis: Glycogen phosphorylase (rate‑limiting) releases glucose‑1‑phosphate; debranching enzyme.
  • Regulation by insulin, glucagon, epinephrine.
6. Pentose Phosphate Pathway
  • Produces NADPH (reductive biosynthesis, antioxidant) and ribose‑5‑phosphate (nucleotide synthesis).
  • Oxidative phase: glucose‑6‑phosphate dehydrogenase (G6PD) – rate‑limiting; deficiency causes hemolytic anemia with oxidative stress.
7. Fatty Acid Oxidation
  • Mitochondrial β‑oxidation: fatty acyl‑CoA → acetyl‑CoA + FADH₂ + NADH.
  • Carnitine shuttle transports long‑chain fatty acids into mitochondria (CPT1, CACT, CPT2).
  • Ketogenesis occurs in liver when acetyl‑CoA accumulates (starvation, diabetes) → acetoacetate, β‑hydroxybutyrate, acetone.
8. Fatty Acid Synthesis
  • Cytosolic; uses acetyl‑CoA (exported as citrate) and NADPH.
  • Acetyl‑CoA carboxylase (ACC) – rate‑limiting, biotin‑dependent, activated by insulin, citrate; inhibited by glucagon, palmitoyl‑CoA.
  • Fatty acid synthase complex produces palmitate.
9. Cholesterol Metabolism
  • Synthesis from acetyl‑CoA via HMG‑CoA reductase (rate‑limiting, inhibited by statins).
  • Cholesterol used for membranes, bile acids, steroid hormones, vitamin D.
10. Lipoprotein Metabolism
  • Chylomicrons: Transport dietary TAG from intestine.
  • VLDL: Transport endogenous TAG from liver.
  • LDL: Cholesterol‑rich; taken up by LDL receptor.
  • HDL: Reverse cholesterol transport.
11. Amino Acid Degradation
  • Transamination (ALT, AST) removes amino group; glutamate carries nitrogen.
  • Oxidative deamination by glutamate dehydrogenase releases NH₃.
12. Urea Cycle
  • Liver mitochondrial and cytosolic; converts toxic NH₃ to urea for excretion.
  • Enzymes: Carbamoyl phosphate synthetase I (CPS I, activator N‑acetylglutamate), ornithine transcarbamylase (OTC, X‑linked deficiency → hyperammonemia), argininosuccinate synthetase, etc.
13. Nucleotide Metabolism
  • Purine synthesis: Starts with ribose‑5‑phosphate → IMP → AMP/GMP. Feedback inhibition by nucleotides.
  • Pyrimidine synthesis: Carbamoyl phosphate (CPS II) → orotate → UMP → CTP, dTMP.
  • Degradation: Purines → uric acid; pyrimidines → β‑alanine, β‑aminoisobutyrate.
14. Citric Acid Cycle
  • Mitochondrial matrix; oxidizes acetyl‑CoA to CO₂, producing 3 NADH, 1 FADH₂, 1 GTP per acetyl‑CoA.
  • Rate‑limiting enzymes: isocitrate dehydrogenase, α‑ketoglutarate dehydrogenase.
15. Electron Transport Chain & Oxidative Phosphorylation
  • Complexes I‑IV; proton pumping creates gradient; ATP synthase produces ATP (chemiosmosis).
  • P/O ratio: number of ATP per oxygen atom; ~2.5 for NADH, ~1.5 for FADH₂.
  • Inhibitors: rotenone (I), antimycin A (III), cyanide/CO (IV), oligomycin (ATP synthase). Uncouplers (dinitrophenol) disrupt gradient.
16. Hormonal Regulation
  • Insulin: Promotes anabolism (glycogen synthesis, glycolysis, lipogenesis, protein synthesis).
  • Glucagon: Catabolic (glycogenolysis, gluconeogenesis, ketogenesis).
  • Epinephrine: Similar to glucagon; mobilizes energy in stress.
  • Cortisol: Gluconeogenesis, protein catabolism.
17. DNA Replication
  • Semiconservative; origin of replication; bidirectional.
  • Enzymes: helicase, primase, DNA polymerase (α, δ, ε), ligase, topoisomerase.
  • Leading strand continuous; lagging strand with Okazaki fragments.
18. Transcription & RNA Processing
  • DNA → RNA by RNA polymerase (I, II, III).
  • Promoters, enhancers, transcription factors.
  • Post‑transcriptional: 5′ cap, 3′ poly‑A tail, splicing (introns removed by spliceosome). Alternative splicing.
19. Translation & Protein Synthesis
  • mRNA → protein on ribosomes (rRNA + proteins).
  • tRNA carries amino acids; codon‑anticodon pairing.
  • Initiation, elongation, termination (release factors).
20. Gene Regulation
  • Prokaryotic: lac operon (inducible), trp operon (repressible).
  • Eukaryotic: transcription factors, chromatin remodeling, DNA methylation, histone acetylation, non‑coding RNAs.
