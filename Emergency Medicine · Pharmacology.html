3. Antiarrhythmics in Emergencies
  • Adenosine: 6 mg rapid IV push, then 12 mg if needed. For stable narrow‑complex SVT (AV nodal reentrant). Mechanism: transient AV nodal blockade.
  • Amiodarone: 150 mg IV over 10 min for stable wide‑complex tachycardia, then infusion.
  • Procainamide: 20‑50 mg/min IV until arrhythmia controlled or hypotension. For atrial fibrillation/flutter, wide‑complex tachycardia.
  • Lidocaine: 1‑1.5 mg/kg IV, then infusion. For ventricular arrhythmias (especially post‑MI).
  • Magnesium: 1‑2 g IV for torsade de pointes, pre‑eclampsia.
4. Rapid Sequence Intubation (RSI) Drugs
RSI combines a sedative (induction agent) and a neuromuscular blocker (paralytic) to facilitate intubation.
Induction Agents
  • Etomidate: 0.3 mg/kg IV – hemodynamically stable; causes adrenal suppression (single dose OK).
  • Ketamine: 1‑2 mg/kg IV – sympathomimetic (↑ BP, HR), good for asthma, shock; dissociative.
  • Propofol: 1‑2 mg/kg IV – rapid onset, hypotension risk; good for neuro cases.
  • Midazolam: 0.2‑0.3 mg/kg IV – slower onset, hypotension; less commonly first‑line.
Neuromuscular Blockers
  • Succinylcholine: 1‑1.5 mg/kg IV – depolarizing, rapid onset (45 sec), duration 5‑10 min. Contraindications: hyperkalemia, muscular dystrophy, malignant hyperthermia.
  • Rocuronium: 0.6‑1.2 mg/kg IV – non‑depolarizing, onset 60‑90 sec, duration 30‑60 min. Can be reversed with sugammadex.
5. Asthma & COPD Drugs
  • Albuterol (salbutamol): Nebulized 2.5‑5 mg q20min or continuous. β2‑agonist bronchodilator.
  • Ipratropium bromide: Nebulized 0.5 mg q20min; anticholinergic, added to β2‑agonists in severe exacerbation.
  • Corticosteroids: Methylprednisolone 125 mg IV or prednisone 40‑60 mg PO. Reduce inflammation.
  • Magnesium sulfate: 2 g IV over 20 min; adjunct in severe asthma.
6. Anticoagulants & Thrombolytics for PE
  • Unfractionated heparin (UFH): 80 U/kg bolus, then 18 U/kg/h drip; monitor aPTT.
  • LMWH (enoxaparin): 1 mg/kg SC q12h; for stable PE.
  • Thrombolytics (alteplase/tPA): 100 mg IV over 2h for massive PE with hypotension.
  • DOACs (rivaroxaban, apixaban): Oral options for stable patients.
7. Acute Coronary Syndrome Drugs
  • Aspirin: 162‑325 mg chewed – antiplatelet.
  • Nitroglycerin: 0.4 mg SL q5min up to 3 doses, then IV for persistent pain/hypertension.
  • Morphine: 2‑4 mg IV for pain unresponsive to nitrates.
  • β‑blockers (metoprolol): 5 mg IV q5min up to 15 mg if no contraindications.
  • Heparin (UFH or LMWH): For NSTEMI.
  • P2Y12 inhibitors: Clopidogrel 300‑600 mg PO, ticagrelor 180 mg PO.
  • GP IIb/IIIa inhibitors (eptifibatide, tirofiban): For high‑risk NSTEMI.
8. Antihypertensives for Emergencies
  • Labetalol: 20 mg IV bolus, then 20‑80 mg q10min up to 300 mg, or drip. α1,β‑blocker.
  • Nicardipine: 5 mg/h IV, titrate q5min; dihydropyridine CCB.
  • Clevidipine: 1‑2 mg/h IV, ultra‑short acting CCB.
  • Nitroprusside: 0.25‑10 µg/kg/min IV – arterial and venous dilator; risk of cyanide toxicity.
  • Hydralazine: 10‑20 mg IV q30min; arteriolar vasodilator, reflex tachycardia.
  • Esmolol: 500 µg/kg IV load, then 50‑300 µg/kg/min; β1‑selective.
9. Aortic Dissection Drugs
  • Goal: Reduce heart rate (to 60 bpm) and BP (to 100‑120 mmHg systolic).
  • β‑blocker first: Esmolol drip or labetolol bolus/drip.
  • Add nitroprusside or nicardipine after β‑blockade to avoid reflex tachycardia.
10. Thrombolytics in Stroke
  • Alteplase (tPA): 0.9 mg/kg IV (max 90 mg), 10% bolus, rest over 1h. Within 3‑4.5h of onset.
  • Exclusion criteria: ICH, recent surgery, severe hypertension, etc.
  • BP control before and after: labetalol, nicardipine.
11. Antiepileptics in Status Epilepticus
  • First‑line (0‑5 min): Benzodiazepines – lorazepam 0.1 mg/kg IV or midazolam 0.2 mg/kg IM/IV.
  • Second‑line (5‑20 min): Fosphenytoin (20 mg PE/kg IV), levetiracetam (60 mg/kg IV), valproate (40 mg/kg IV).
  • Third‑line (refractory): Continuous infusion midazolam, propofol, pentobarbital; with EEG monitoring.
12. Drugs for Increased ICP
  • Mannitol: 0.5‑1 g/kg IV bolus; osmotic diuretic.
  • Hypertonic saline (3%): 150‑300 mL bolus; alternative to mannitol.
  • Elevate head, hyperventilation (temporary), sedation, paralytics, barbiturates.
13. Electrolyte Emergencies Management
  • Hyperkalemia:
    • Stabilize myocardium: calcium gluconate 1‑2 g IV.
    • Shift K⁺: insulin 10 units + D50 25g, albuterol nebulized, sodium bicarbonate if acidotic.
    • Remove: kayexalate, dialysis.
  • Hypokalemia: IV/PO potassium (max 10‑20 mEq/h).
  • Hyponatremia: Severe symptoms (seizures): 3% hypertonic saline 100‑150 mL bolus; correct slowly (<8 mEq/L/day).
  • Hypercalcemia: IV fluids, bisphosphonates (pamidronate, zoledronic acid), calcitonin.

15. Endocrine Emergencies
  • DKA: IV fluids, insulin infusion (0.1 U/kg/h), K⁺ replacement, bicarbonate rarely.
  • HHS: Aggressive fluid resuscitation, insulin, K⁺.
  • Thyroid storm: β‑blockers (propranolol), thionamides (PTU, methimazole), iodine, steroids, cooling.
  • Myxedema coma: IV levothyroxine (or liothyronine), steroids, supportive care.
16. Analgesics in Emergency
  • Opioids: Morphine (2‑5 mg IV), fentanyl (25‑50 µg IV), hydromorphone (0.2‑0.5 mg IV).
  • Ketamine: 0.1‑0.3 mg/kg IV for sub‑dissociative analgesia; also for dissociative sedation.
  • NSAIDs: Ketorolac 15‑30 mg IV/IM for moderate pain.
  • Acetaminophen: 1 g IV/PO.
17. Procedural Sedation
  • Midazolam: 0.05‑0.1 mg/kg IV – benzodiazepine, amnestic.
  • Fentanyl: 0.5‑1 µg/kg IV – opioid, synergistic with midazolam.
  • Propofol: 0.5‑1 mg/kg IV, then 0.5 mg/kg q3min – rapid onset, hypotension risk.
  • Ketamine: 1‑2 mg/kg IV – dissociative, maintains airway reflexes.
  • Etomidate: 0.1‑0.15 mg/kg IV – brief sedation, minimal hemodynamic effect.
18. Antibiotics in Sepsis
  • Broad‑spectrum empiric therapy within 1 hour.
  • Community‑acquired: Ceftriaxone + azithromycin (or respiratory fluoroquinolone).
  • Health‑care associated: Anti‑pseudomonal (cefepime, meropenem, piperacillin‑tazobactam) + vancomycin for MRSA.
  • Adjust based on cultures and de‑escalate.
19. Antibiotics for Meningitis
  • Empiric (adults): Ceftriaxone 2g IV q12h + vancomycin + dexamethasone (adjunct).
  • If immunocompromised or >50: Add ampicillin (for Listeria).
  • Neonates: Ampicillin + cefotaxime + acyclovir (if HSV).
20. Hyperthermia & Malignant Hyperthermia
  • Heat stroke: Rapid cooling (evaporative, cold IV fluids), benzodiazepines for shivering.
  • Malignant hyperthermia: Dantrolene 2.5 mg/kg IV q5min up to 10 mg/kg; cooling, bicarbonate, treat hyperkalemia.
21. Hypothermia
  • Rewarming techniques (passive, active external, active internal).
  • Warm IV fluids, warmed humidified oxygen.
  • Avoid vasopressors if possible; CPR if needed.
