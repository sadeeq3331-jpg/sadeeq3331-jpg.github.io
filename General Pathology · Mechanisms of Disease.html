1. Single‑Gene Disorders
Caused by mutations in a single gene; follow Mendelian inheritance patterns.
Autosomal Dominant
  • Huntington disease, Neurofibromatosis type 1, Marfan syndrome, Familial hypercholesterolemia.
Autosomal Recessive
  • Cystic fibrosis, Sickle cell disease, Tay‑Sachs, Phenylketonuria.
X‑Linked
  • Duchenne muscular dystrophy, Hemophilia A, G6PD deficiency.

3. Multifactorial Inheritance
  • Interaction of multiple genes + environment.
  • Examples: Neural tube defects, cleft lip/palate, diabetes, hypertension, coronary artery disease.

5. Autoimmune Diseases
  • Organ‑specific: Hashimoto thyroiditis, Graves, type 1 diabetes, Addison, myasthenia gravis.
  • Systemic: SLE, RA, Sjögren, systemic sclerosis.
6. Immunodeficiency Syndromes
  • Primary: SCID, DiGeorge, Bruton, CVID, IgA deficiency, CGD, LAD.
  • Secondary: HIV/AIDS, malnutrition, immunosuppressive drugs.
7. Pathogenesis of Infection
  • Entry (skin, mucosal, parenteral).
  • Adherence (pili, adhesins).
  • Evasion of host defenses (capsule, antigenic variation).
  • Damage by toxins (exotoxins, endotoxin) or host response.
8. Viral Infections
  • DNA viruses: HPV, HBV, HSV, CMV, EBV, Adenovirus.
  • RNA viruses: Influenza, HIV, HCV, Polio, Rabies, Measles.
  • Cytopathic effects: Inclusion bodies, syncytia, lysis.
  • Latency (HSV, VZV) and oncogenesis (HPV, HBV, EBV).
9. Bacterial Infections
  • Gram‑positive cocci: Staph, Strep, Enterococcus.
  • Gram‑negative rods: E. coli, Salmonella, Pseudomonas.
  • Acid‑fast: Mycobacterium TB, leprae.
  • Spirochetes: Treponema, Borrelia, Leptospira.
  • Intracellular: Chlamydia, Rickettsia, Listeria.
10. Fungal Infections
  • Superficial: Dermatophytes, Candida.
  • Systemic: Histoplasma, Blastomyces, Coccidioides, Cryptococcus.
  • Opportunistic: Aspergillus, Candida, Mucor (in immunocompromised).
11. Parasitic Infections
  • Protozoa: Malaria, Toxoplasma, Giardia, Entamoeba.
  • Helminths: Ascaris, Hookworm, Taenia, Schistosoma.
12. Chemical & Physical Injury
  • Heavy metals: Lead (anemia, neuropathy), Mercury (neurologic), Arsenic (skin cancer).
  • Industrial chemicals: CCl₄ (liver necrosis), Benzene (leukemia).
  • Physical agents: Radiation (DNA damage), trauma, thermal injury.
13. Nutritional Disorders
  • Protein‑energy malnutrition: Marasmus, Kwashiorkor.
  • Vitamin deficiencies: A (night blindness), B1 (beriberi), B3 (pellagra), C (scurvy), D (rickets), K (bleeding).
  • Overnutrition: Obesity, metabolic syndrome.
14. Atherosclerosis
  • Response‑to‑injury hypothesis: Endothelial injury → lipid accumulation (LDL) → inflammation → smooth muscle proliferation → fibrous cap.
  • Risk factors: Dyslipidemia, hypertension, smoking, diabetes, age.
  • Complications: MI, stroke, peripheral artery disease.
15. Cirrhosis
  • Diffuse fibrosis with regenerative nodules.
  • Etiologies: Alcohol, viral hepatitis (B, C), NASH, autoimmune, biliary.
  • Complications: Portal hypertension, ascites, varices, encephalopathy, HCC.
16. Diabetes Mellitus
  • Type 1: Autoimmune destruction of β‑cells (absolute insulin deficiency).
  • Type 2: Insulin resistance + relative deficiency.
  • Complications: Microvascular (retinopathy, nephropathy, neuropathy), macrovascular (CAD, stroke).
17. Amyloidosis
  • Extracellular deposition of fibrillar proteins (β‑pleated sheet).
  • AL: Immunoglobulin light chains (multiple myeloma).
  • AA: Serum amyloid A (chronic inflammation).
  • ATTR: Transthyretin (senile, familial).
  • Stain: Congo red → apple‑green birefringence.
